5 Most Important Rules

  • Team Composition: Each team must have five players on the court. Substitutions can be made during stoppages in play.
  • Game Duration: Professional games are played in four quarters of 12 minutes each, while college games have two halves of 20 minutes each.
  • Scoring Points: A basket made from inside the three-point arc earns 2 points, while a successful shot from beyond the arc scores 3 points. Free throws are worth 1 point each.
  • Ball Handling: Players must dribble the ball while moving. Picking up the ball and moving without dribbling is considered "traveling" and results in a turnover.
  • Fouls and Penalties: Personal fouls occur when illegal physical contact is made. Accumulating too many fouls can lead to free throws for the opposing team or even disqualification of a player.

Did You Know? The NBA introduced the shot clock in 1954 to prevent teams from stalling. The clock requires teams to take a shot within 24 seconds, adding excitement and pace to the game.
